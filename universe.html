The Universe & Its Profound Mysteries

From the infinitely small to the unimaginably large, the universe is a tapestry woven with phenomena that defy easy explanation. It challenges our understanding of space, time, matter, and energy. Join us in contemplating some of the grandest questions humanity asks about the cosmos.

Hubble Ultra Deep Field

Staring into a tiny patch of sky, Hubble revealed nearly 10,000 galaxies, some dating back near the beginning of time, offering a profound look into cosmic history.

Credit: NASA, ESA, S. Beckwith (STScI) and the HUDF Team
Pillars of Creation (Eagle Nebula)

Iconic columns of cool interstellar hydrogen gas and dust acting as nurseries for new stars. These majestic structures are slowly being eroded by intense radiation from nearby stars.

Credit: NASA, ESA/Hubble and the Hubble Heritage Team
Cosmic Microwave Background

The faint afterglow radiation from the Big Bang, providing a snapshot of the universe in its infancy. Studying its tiny temperature fluctuations reveals clues about cosmic evolution.

Credit: ESA and the Planck Collaboration
Dark Matter Structure

Invisible dark matter forms the cosmic web, a vast scaffolding upon which galaxies and galaxy clusters form. Its gravitational influence shapes the universe's large-scale structure.

Credit: Springel et al. (MPIA) & Illustris Collaboration

Unsolved Cosmic Conundrums

  • Dark Matter & Dark Energy: What are these invisible components that constitute ~95% of the universe's total mass-energy budget? Their nature remains one of the biggest puzzles in physics. [Learn More]
  • Black Hole Singularities: What happens at the center of a black hole where gravity becomes infinite and known laws of physics break down?
  • The Origin & Fate of the Universe: While the Big Bang model is well-supported, what precisely triggered it? And will the universe expand forever, collapse, or tear itself apart?
  • The Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry: Why is the universe dominated by matter when the Big Bang should have produced equal amounts of matter and antimatter?
  • Are We Alone? (The Fermi Paradox): Given the vastness of the universe and the probability of Earth-like planets, why haven't we detected any signs of extraterrestrial intelligence?
  • The Nature of Time: Is time fundamental, or an emergent property? Does it always flow forward?
